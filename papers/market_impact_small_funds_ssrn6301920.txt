Market Impact Models for Small Funds: Practical Execution Alpha, Intraday Liquidity Dynamics, and the Microstructure of Trading Cost
Divyanshu Verma
Verma Research Capital (VRC)
SSRN: 6301920

ABSTRACT
For a small hedge fund, execution is not an operational afterthought but a primary source of edge that compounds silently across every trade. This paper develops a comprehensive quantitative framework for understanding, measuring, and minimising market impact in the context of small fund operations (10-200M USD scale).

1. STRUCTURAL MODELS OF MARKET IMPACT

A. Square-Root Law
I(Q) = η · σ · sqrt(Q/V)
- η: dimensionless constant ~0.5 to 1.0
- Q: trade size, V: average daily volume, σ: daily volatility
- Total daily impact (N trades): N · η · σ · sqrt(Q/V) · P
- Implication: cumulative impact across many small trades is material even when individual impact is tiny

B. Almgren-Chriss Optimal Execution Framework
Trade-off: executing too slowly (price risk) vs. too quickly (market impact)
Three cost components:
1. Permanent cost = γ · X² (independent of schedule)
2. Temporary cost = η · Σ(x_k²/Δt)
3. Risk cost = λ · σ² · Δt · Σ(X̄_k²)
Optimal strategy: x_k* = X · sinh(κ(T-t_k)) / sinh(κT) · κΔt
where κ = sqrt(λσ²/η) = urgency parameter
Key insight: optimal execution speed is dynamic, not fixed.

C. Transient Impact (Gatheral 2010)
ΔP_t = ∫ G(t-s) · ẋ_s ds, where G(τ) = λe^(-ρτ)
- Price pressure decays exponentially with rate ρ
- Self-impact cost for mean-reversion strategies
- Optimal rebalancing frequency: f* = f_signal · min(1, sqrt(ρσ²/η·f_signal·q̄²))

2. INTRADAY LIQUIDITY DYNAMICS

A. U-Shaped Intraday Pattern
S(t) = S_min + (S_open - S_min)·e^(-α₁t) + (S_close - S_min)·e^(-α₂(T-t))
- Spreads widest at open and close, narrowest mid-session
- Volume highest at open and close

B. Intraday Depth
D_eff(Q,c) = Σ q_p for |p - mid| ≤ c · mid/10000
- Monitor effective depth in real time

C. Short-Term Volatility
VolRatio_t = σ̂_{t,5min} / σ̄_{5min}
- VolRatio > 1.5: defer non-urgent executions

3. VRC EXECUTION STATE MODEL (ESM)

Four inputs:
1. Relative bid-ask spread: S̃_t = S_t / S̄
2. Relative order book depth: D̃_t = D_t^eff / D̄^eff
3. Short-term volatility ratio: VolRatio_t
4. Signed order flow imbalance: OFI_t

EQS_t = 0.30·Ŝ_t + 0.25·(2-D̃_t) + 0.25·VolRatio_t + 0.20·|OFI_t|

Regimes:
- Liquid (EQS < 0.8): full size, market/limit at spread
- Normal (0.8-1.2): passive limits, 90% of target
- Thin (1.2-1.6): reduce urgency, 70% of target
- Stressed (>1.6): only if time-critical, 40% of target

Regime-conditioned cost:
Ĉ(Q, regime) = S_t/2 + η(regime)·σ_t·sqrt(Q/V) + 1[Q>D_eff]·ξ·(Q-D_eff)²/D_eff

4. EQUITY MARKET EXECUTION
- True cost example: 2M position, 2.9% of ADV → ~7 bps one-way, ~14 bps round-trip = 35% of gross alpha consumed
- Passive vs. Aggressive: prefer passive when P(fill) > Δp/(Δp + S/2)
- At-minimum-tick stocks: different order book dynamics

5. INDEX FUTURES EXECUTION
- Best liquidity: US equity open (9:30-10:30 AM ET) and cash close (3:30-4:00 PM)
- Roll cost reduction: roll 1-2 weeks early or 1 week late → saves 3-8 bps per roll (~12-32 bps/year)
- Optimal rebalancing < signal frequency when transient decay is slow

6. OPTIONS EXECUTION
C_option = S_IV·V/2 + κ_IV·D/(V·OI_{K,T})
- Liquidity-adjusted fair value: V_adj(K) = V_BS(K) - C_option(K)
- Event-driven spread: S_IV(τ) = S̄_IV·(1 + ψ·e^(-μτ))
- Rule: no options positions within 15 min of Tier-1 announcements

7. PATH-DEPENDENT ASYMMETRY (Entry vs. Exit)
E[C_total] = Σ P(entry in r₁, exit in r₂)·[C(Q,r₁) + C(Q,r₂)]
- Q_adj = Q_naive · (μ - C_entry - E[C_exit|adverse]) / (μ - 2C̃)

8. EXECUTION ALPHA
α_exec = 2 · δC · N · P·Q/NAV
Example: 500 trades/yr, 4 bps improvement → 16 bps/year execution alpha
SR_new = SR_old + α_exec/σ₀
- Execution alpha has near-zero volatility → disproportionate Sharpe improvement
- Abstention is optimal when μ_signal < C_entry + E[C_exit]

9. POSITION SIZING
Kelly with execution costs: f* = (μ - C_total)/σ²
Liquidity-adjusted max size: Q_max^liq = V · τ_max · p_max
- VRC caps: 5% ADV for equities, 2% for index futures (non-peak hours)

10. VRC OPPORTUNISTIC EXECUTION ALGORITHM (OEA)
w(EQS_t) = (2 - EQS_t)^β / Σ(2 - EQS_s)^β
- Front-loads execution in best-quality windows
- Falls back to aggressive execution at T_max deadline
